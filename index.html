About the Ward

Location & Geography
Etymology
Population

Chililabombwe (formerly Bancroft) is a mining town in Zambia’s Copperbelt Province, near the DRC border, at 1,360 m above sea level.

The name Chililabombwe translates to “place of the croaking frog” in Lamba language.

The town had around 90,530 residents (2010), while the district had ~141,899 residents.

FAQ

What are the must-visit attractions in Chililabombwe?

Popular attractions include local mines, community parks, and cultural events.

What is the best time to visit?

The dry season (May to October) is ideal for visiting.

Is Chililabombwe safe for visitors?

Yes, standard precautions are recommended.

Transportation options?

Buses, taxis, and private transport are available.

Local delicacies to try?

Try nshima, fried fish, and local vegetable dishes.

Cultural festivals in Chililabombwe?

Festivals include traditional dances, music events, and mining celebrations.
